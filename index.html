The Essence of Blue.

Deep Blues and Deeper Meanings in Picasso's Artistic Journey

The Blue Period represents a time in Picasso's life marred by despair and destitution. Sparked by the suicide of his close friend, Carlos Casagemas, Picasso's works during this period reflect his own emotional turmoil and sympathy for the plight of the marginalized in society.

Le bock

Le bock

A 1901 oil on canvas by Pablo Picasso, it captures his friend Jaime Sabartés in Paris during the early Blue Period. Displayed in the Pushkin Museum, this piece is one of eight Sabartés portraits, repurposing a canvas originally depicting a seated child.

La soupe

La soupe

An evocative piece from Picasso's Blue Period, it portrays a tender yet stark moment between a woman and child, set against a backdrop of hardship. This poignant work, reflecting themes of poverty and compassion, remains a testament to Picasso's mastery in capturing deep emotions with minimal yet powerful strokes.

La Vie

La Vie

created in Barcelona, May 1903, it is a striking 196.5 x 129.2 cm work portraying two couples and a solitary figure, echoing themes of Sorrow by Vincent van Gogh. Notable for its repainted canvas, originally 'The derniers Moments' from 1899, it quickly sold to French art dealer Jean Saint Gaudens despite Picasso's financial struggles.
